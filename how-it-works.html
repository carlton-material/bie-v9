Signal Synthesis Methodology
Composite = Σ(Human_i × 0.33 + Behavioral_i × 0.33 + Cultural_i × 0.33) → Unified Intelligence Score
Human-Expressive signals capture perception and emotion through NLP analysis of N=4,700 AI-moderated interviews. Signal synthesis uses a consensus-weighted engine that detects convergence and conflict across all three layers, producing a resilient intelligence output with documented confidence at every step.
Key Assumptions
01 Three imperfect signal types produce more robust intelligence than one clean signal
02 Signal convergence across types indicates higher confidence than volume alone
03 Behavioral signals lag attitudinal shifts by 2-4 weeks; synthesis engine accounts for temporal offset

These assumptions are transparent because they're testable. If behavioral signals start leading attitudinal shifts, or if single-signal confidence outperforms convergence, we'll detect the mismatch and update the model. This is not a static system; it learns.

03. Methodology

From Signals to Intelligence

Every signal passes through a standardized pipeline: ingest raw data, normalize to comparable units, correlate across layers, score confidence, and surface insights. Each step is documented. Each assumption is testable. Glass Box principle: every insight traces to its source.

Step 1
Ingest
Collect raw signals from 847+ daily data streams. Volume ensures outliers don't distort.
Step 2
Normalize
Convert diverse signals to comparable units (0–1 propensity scores, indices, vectors).
Step 3
Correlate
Cross-layer analysis: do human, behavioral, and cultural signals align or conflict?
Step 4
Score
Calculate confidence intervals. Document sample size, recency, convergence strength.
Step 5
Surface
Deliver intelligence across 7 outputs. Every metric shows source and confidence level.

Confidence Calculation
Confidence = f(Signal_Convergence, Sample_N, Data_Recency) → HIGH | MEDIUM | LOW
Confidence is not subjective. It is calculated from three objective criteria: (1) how many signal types converge on the same direction, (2) sample size relative to population, and (3) data recency relative to market cycle speed. Every claim in the platform displays its confidence score and the criteria behind it. No exceptions.

Brand Fidelity Composite Methodology
BF Composite = Σ(Driver_i × Weight_i) / Σ(Weight_i)
Weighted composite of 6 Brand Fidelity drivers measured via mixed-method signal integration. In-the-Moment drivers (User Friendly, Personal, Accessible) weighted 45%, Over-Time drivers (Dependable, Meaningful, Salient) weighted 55%. Each driver is continuously measured by its signal layer. When any driver shifts, the composite updates automatically.
